My Projects


iWorlds

iWorlds is a multi-player educational space simulation company that incorporates learning and technology in a team building environment.

Classrooms were retrofitted you look like the inside of a space ship. Imagine being on the bridge of the star trek voyager where you manage the ships resources through your console while you explore the stars.

Over 300,000 students have gone through these simulations.

Technologies Used: Python, Java, Swing, Ogre3D


Nasa Rover Competition

I competed with a team in the nasa rover competition. The competition involves a simulated extra-planetary rover mission where the robot needs to traverse varying difficulties of terrain while collecting and storing rock samples.

The project is sponsored by the College of Engineering at the University of Utah comprised of undergraduates and postgraduates.

I developed communication interface that allowed the robot to be driven with an Xbox controller over a 4g network.

Our team took 2nd place at the competition.

Technologies Used: Beagle Board, C++, Potentiometer, Robot Operating System


HipGeo

HipGeo was a blogging and social networking app for travel. You could use the app to passively track your route on trips and intelligently collect your media and text uploads into replayable trip posts.

The social elements let you see what's going on around you as well as keep you up to date on your friends' adventures.

I worked on the iPhone app along with one other team member. HipGeo was featured in Forbes, TechCrunch, PCMag, ReadWriteWeb, TUAW, and GigaOM.

Technologies Used: Android SDK, PhP, Amazon Web Services, Android NDK, FFMPEG


Canvas for Android

Canvas is a learning management system for universities and schools. It allows students to create class discussions, submit homework, and check up on their grades.

Today, more than 2,000 universities, school districts and institutions around the world use Canvas reaching over 10 million students, teachers, and instructors.

Technologies Used: Android SDK, PhP, Amazon Web Services, Android NDK, FFMPEG
